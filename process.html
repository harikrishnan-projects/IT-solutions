✅ Research Phase

Discover & Plan — What to include

Each step below explains what we do, why it matters, typical activities, and common deliverables so clients know what to expect.

  1. 1

    Requirement Gathering

    Collect concrete functional and non-functional needs from stakeholders and users so the scope is well-defined.

    • Stakeholder interviews: Meet product owners, business users, and ops to capture expectations.
    • User research: Surveys or quick interviews to learn user goals and pain points.
    • Existing assets review: Audit current systems, APIs, data models, and documentation.
    • Constraints capture: Budget, timeline, compliance, and platform limitations.

    Deliverables: Requirements doc (priority backlog), personas summary, and an initial scope list.

  2. 2

    Understanding Client Goals

    Ensure alignment on business outcomes and success metrics so every feature ties back to a measurable goal.

    • Define success metrics: KPIs like conversion rate, time-to-task, or cost-savings.
    • Prioritization workshop: MoSCoW or RICE exercises to rank features by impact and effort.
    • Stakeholder sign-off: Agreement on MVP vs. future phases and acceptance criteria.

    Deliverables: Goals & KPIs doc, prioritized feature list, and acceptance criteria checklist.

  3. 3

    Project Analysis

    Study technical feasibility, risks, integrations, and dependencies to reduce surprises during development.

    • Feasibility study: Prototype key flows or validate third-party APIs quickly.
    • Risk assessment: Identify security, performance, and operational risks with mitigation steps.
    • Data & integration mapping: Diagram data flows and touchpoints with external systems.
    • Estimation: Rough effort estimates (T-shirt or story points) for planning purposes.

    Deliverables: Risk log, integration diagrams, feasibility notes, and rough estimates.

  4. 4

    Technology Selection

    Choose frameworks, hosting, and tools that best meet the goals, team skills, and constraints.

    • Evaluate options: Compare frontend, backend, database, and infra candidates by tradeoffs.
    • Team fit: Prefer technologies your team can support or hire for quickly.
    • Scalability & maintenance: Consider long-term costs and operability (CI/CD, monitoring).
    • Licensing & vendor risk: Check open-source licenses and vendor lock-in exposure.

    Deliverables: Tech-stack recommendation, rationale doc, and a short POC (if needed).

  5. 5

    Roadmap Creation

    Translate priorities into an actionable phased plan with milestones, sprints, and review checkpoints.

    • Phase definition: Define MVP, phase 2, and long-term enhancements.
    • Milestones & timelines: Set delivery windows, demo dates, and feedback loops.
    • Team resourcing: Assign roles and identify hiring/training needs.
    • Acceptance & launch plan: Define release criteria, rollout strategy, and post-launch support.

    Deliverables: Roadmap timeline (Gantt or swimlane), sprint plan for MVP, and launch checklist.
